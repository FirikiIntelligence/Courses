## **The Battle of the Recognition: Comparing Cybersecurity Certifications**  

Choosing the right cybersecurity certification can feel like a battle. Below, Iâ€™ve broken down the **pros and cons** of popular and niche certifications, of my personal choice, to help me & you decide which one is right for me & you.  

---

### **1. OSCP (Offensive Security Certified Professional)**  
**Pros:**  
- **Industry Gold Standard:** Highly respected for penetration testing roles.  
- **Hands-On Exam:** 24-hour practical test with a real-world focus.  
- **Strong Foundation:** Covers networking, Linux, scripting, and exploitation.  
- **Career Boost:** Opens doors to entry-level offensive security roles.  

**Cons:**  
- **Costly:** ~$1,500 (exam + training).  
- **Steep Learning Curve:** Requires prior scripting/networking knowledge.  
- **Stressful Exam:** Time-limited and challenging for beginners.  

**Best For:** Students aiming for penetration testing careers who can invest time and money.  

---

### **2. CRTO (Certified Red Team Operator)**  
**Pros:**  
- **Red Team Focus:** Simulates real-world attacks (Cobalt Strike, Azure/M365).  
- **Affordable:** ~$500 (exam + lab access).  
- **Self-Paced:** Flexible for students.  

**Cons:**  
- **Niche Recognition:** Less known outside red team circles.  
- **Requires Basics:** Assumes familiarity with C2 frameworks.  

**Best For:** Students interested in **corporate red teaming** or Azure/M365 attacks.  

---

### **3. PNPT (Practical Network Penetration Tester)**  
**Pros:**  
- **Beginner-Friendly:** Covers OSINT, network attacks, and reporting.  
- **Affordable:** ~$400 (exam + course).  
- **Real-World Exam:** 5-day take-home assessment.  

**Cons:**  
- **Newer Cert:** Less recognition than OSCP.  

**Best For:** Budget-conscious students wanting practical, entry-level pentesting skills.  

---

### **4. CPTS (Certified Penetration Testing Specialist)**  
**Pros:**  
- **HTB Labs Access:** Includes 200+ hands-on modules.  
- **Comprehensive:** Covers web apps, networks, and Active Directory.  
- **Self-Paced:** Flexible learning.  

**Cons:**  
- **Very New:** Limited industry recognition (launched in 2022).  

**Best For:** Students who want hands-on labs and a structured learning path.  

---

### **5. MRT - Certified Red Teamer**  
**Info:**  
- The **Mosse Institute** offers specialized certifications in hacking and red teaming. The MRT is marketed as a red teaming certification but is **not widely recognized** in the industry.  
- Limited information is available about its content or quality.  

**Pros:**  
- **Specialized Content:** Likely focuses on practical red teaming (depending on the curriculum).  
- **Affordable:** May be cheaper than mainstream certifications.  

**Cons:**  
- **Unknown Value:** Due to lack of industry recognition.  
- **Unclear Framework:** No clear data on training or exam quality.  

**Best For:** Students exploring red teaming on a budget, but **not** as a primary career certification.  

---

### **6. CNPen (Certified Network Pentester)**  
**Info:**  
- Focuses on **network penetration testing**, covering internal/external network exploitation, pivoting, and lateral movement.  
- Likely includes hands-on labs simulating real-world network environments.  

**Pros:**  
- **Hands-On Focus:** Emphasizes practical skills over theory.  
- **Beginner-Friendly:** Step-by-step labs for newcomers.  
- **Affordable:** ~$400.  

**Cons:**  
- **Limited Recognition:** New and niche, lacking industry-wide credibility.  
- **Unclear Exam Rigor:** Few reviews to validate difficulty or quality.  

**Best For:** Beginner network pentesting.  

---

### **8. CAPE (Certified Active Directory Pentesting Expert)**  
**Info:**  
- Focuses on **advanced Active Directory pentesting**, including Kerberos attacks, AD misconfigurations, and lateral movement.  
- Likely includes hands-on labs and simulated enterprise AD environments.  

**Pros:**  
- **AD Specialization:** Deep focus on Active Directory exploitation.

**Cons:**

### **9. CAPE (Certified Adversarial Path Emulation - SpecterOps)**
